A baby girl was named Rapunzel.
She was taken by a sorceress.
The sorceress raised her in a tall tower.
There was no door near the ground.
Only a small window opened to the sky.
Rapunzel's hair grew bright and long.
When the sorceress visited, she called upward.
Rapunzel, Rapunzel, let down your hair.
The golden braid fell like a rope.
One day a prince heard Rapunzel sing.
He hid and learned the secret call.
Soon he climbed the shining hair.
They spoke often and planned escape.
But the sorceress discovered the visits.
In anger she cut off Rapunzel's hair.
She sent the girl far away.
Then she tricked the prince at the tower.
He fell and was badly hurt.
For a long time he wandered in sorrow.
At last he heard Rapunzel's voice again.
They met in a lonely place.
Her tears healed his eyes in many versions.
Together they left the years of captivity.
Freedom became the true end of the tale.
