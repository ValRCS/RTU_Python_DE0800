A girl wore a red hood every day.
People called her Little Red Riding Hood.
One morning her mother filled a basket.
She was to visit her sick grandmother.
The path through the forest was quiet.
Her mother warned her not to wander.
But the girl stopped to pick flowers.
A wolf watched from the trees.
He spoke with a friendly voice.
She told him where Grandmother lived.
The wolf hurried by a shorter path.
He reached the cottage before the girl.
Grandmother opened the door and gasped.
The wolf hid her and climbed into bed.
Soon the girl arrived with the basket.
She noticed large ears and bright eyes.
The wolf sprang from the blanket.
In many tellings a woodcutter hears the noise.
He rushes in with a strong axe.
Grandmother and the girl are saved.
The wolf is driven from the house.
The girl remembers her mother's warning.
She learns that sweet words can hide danger.
The red hood is worn more wisely after that.
