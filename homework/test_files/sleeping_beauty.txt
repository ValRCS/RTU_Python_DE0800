A princess was born to great joy.
The king and queen held a feast.
Fairies came with blessings and gifts.
But one angry fairy was not invited.
She placed a cruel curse on the child.
Before sunset on her sixteenth birthday,
the princess would prick her finger and die.
Another fairy softened the dark promise.
The princess would sleep instead of dying.
Many years passed in careful peace.
Spinning wheels were hidden from the castle.
Yet fate found a narrow path.
The princess climbed an old tower.
There she found a woman with a spindle.
She touched the point and fell asleep.
At once the whole castle slept.
Thorns and roses grew around the walls.
Time moved quietly beyond the hedge.
At last a prince made his way through.
He found the silent hall and the sleeping court.
When he reached the princess, the spell ended.
Eyes opened across the castle.
The cook, the dogs, and the guards all woke.
The long sleep gave way to music and light.
