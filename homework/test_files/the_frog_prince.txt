A princess loved a golden ball.
She played with it beside a cool spring.
One day the ball slipped from her hands.
It fell into the dark water.
She began to cry by the edge.
A frog lifted his head above the pool.
He offered help for a promise.
She said he could share her meals and company.
The frog brought the golden ball back.
Then the princess hurried away.
That evening the frog came to the palace.
He knocked at the great door.
The king asked what she had promised.
He told her that a promise must be kept.
Reluctantly she let the frog inside.
In some versions she throws him in anger.
In other versions she grows kinder with time.
Either way the spell soon breaks.
The frog becomes a prince again.
An evil enchantment had changed his form.
The princess sees him with new eyes.
They speak honestly at last.
The palace celebrates the strange turn of fate.
The tale remembers both duty and transformation.
