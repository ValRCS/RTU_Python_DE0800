In summer a duck nested by the reeds.
Her eggs were warm beneath her wings.
At last the shells began to crack.
Several yellow ducklings stepped into the light.
One last egg opened very slowly.
The bird inside looked large and gray.
The others called him ugly.
He heard laughter wherever he walked.
Even the hens pecked at him.
Sad and lonely, he ran away.
He crossed fields and marshes alone.
Autumn winds blew over the cold water.
He saw graceful swans flying south.
Something stirred in his heart.
Winter covered the ponds with ice.
He barely survived the bitter season.
When spring returned, the world softened.
He found the swans on a shining lake.
He feared they would mock him too.
But in the water he saw his reflection.
He was no duckling at all.
He had grown into a young swan.
The birds welcomed him with quiet grace.
At last he understood his true nature.
