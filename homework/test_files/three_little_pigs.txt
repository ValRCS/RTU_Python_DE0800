Three little pigs left home together.
Each wanted to build a house.
The first pig chose straw.
He finished quickly and played all day.
The second pig chose sticks.
His house looked stronger but was still light.
The third pig chose bricks.
He worked slowly and carefully.
Soon a hungry wolf came by.
He knocked on the straw house.
The first pig refused to open the door.
So the wolf huffed and puffed.
The straw house flew apart.
The first pig ran to his brother.
Then the wolf blew down the stick house too.
Both pigs fled to the brick house.
The wolf snarled and blew with all his might.
But the brick walls did not shake.
He tried the chimney instead.
The wise pig had prepared a pot below.
The wolf leaped down and yelped in pain.
After that he ran far away.
The three pigs learned the value of care.
Quick work is not always strong work.
